Thesis: Event Study on Litigation Risk and Firm Valuation

My Thesis topic: How do board characteristics influence the likelihood of sustainability-related litigation in fashion industry firms, and what is the impact of such litigation on firm valuation?

  • Conducted a thesis research project performing an event study on fashion companies including H&M, Nike, Lululemon, Adidas, Ralph Lauren, and Burberry.
  • Calculated abnormal returns (ARs) and cumulative abnormal returns (CARs) using Stata to assess market reactions to sustainability-related litigation.
  • Analyzed financial data across multiple event windows: [-1,+1], [-3,+3], [-5,+5], and [-10,+10].
  • Found that sustainability lawsuits and controversies typically cause short-term share price drops, with partial recovery over time.
  • Identified the influence of board composition, showing that companies with more women on boards often face closer scrutiny.
  • Determined that ESG committees and sustainability experts do not consistently prevent legal or reputational risks, highlighting gaps in governance effectiveness.
  • Concluded that many corporate governance measures remain symbolic rather than truly effective in mitigating ESG-related risks.
  • Highlighted that strengthening board accountability and enhancing regulatory enforcement can improve ESG effectiveness in the fashion industry.
  • Developed expertise in financial data analysis, event study methodology, and translating quantitative results into actionable regulatory and strategic insights.
